The Fictional
Brand Launch

Aarka Studio Brand Identity Newsletter Bengaluru

"Build us a brand identity for our first client mailer. We have nothing yet."

Brand: Aarka Studio — Boutique workplace design consultancy, Bengaluru

The Scenario

Aarka Studio is two architects who left a large firm to do things differently. Their clients are Series B startups and mid-size tech companies who want beautiful, functional offices without a 6-month timeline. They are about to send their first newsletter to 200 potential clients. They have no logo, no colour palette, no brand voice. They have a name, a belief, and a list of 200 email addresses.

"Good design does not need a big budget."

Deliverables
→

A logo mark or wordmark — one direction only, no variations needed. Show us where you started, not where you could go.

→

A colour palette with 3–4 colours and one line explaining each choice. The line is as important as the colour.

→

The first newsletter — fully designed, real copy, ready to send. Subject line included.

→

One additional brand touchpoint of your choice. Make the choice matter.

Also Answer
What was the first design decision you made for Aarka — and what did you deliberately avoid? +
Write your answer here. The first decision is rarely the logo. What was yours?
The newsletter subject line: what made you choose it? +
Write your answer here. Subject lines are strategy disguised as copywriting.
We Are Testing Brand thinking from zero, newsletter design, and whether your collateral choices are intentional or accidental.

Brand: Maara Spaces — Co-working and managed office brand

The Scenario

Maara Spaces is launching in three Indian cities. They are shooting a 60-second brand film — no voiceover, music-led, meant for Instagram Reels and YouTube pre-roll. The shoot is in 6 days. The director has the location and crew. What they do not have is a visual direction.

Key Constraint

You will not be on set. You need to hand off everything in a single document the director can execute without calling you. If they have to call you, you haven't done your job.

Deliverables
→

A mood board: 6–8 visual references, each labelled with one line explaining why it's there. Not a Pinterest dump — a directorial argument.

→

On-screen text design: font, size, placement, timing. Show it in context of a frame.

→

The YouTube thumbnail design. This is the first frame anyone sees before they press play.

Also Answer
What is the single visual idea that holds the whole film together? +
Write your answer here. Not a mood, not a vibe — an idea. One sentence.
What did you decide NOT to show in this film, and why? +
Write your answer here. Restraint is a creative decision too.
We Are Testing Visual direction thinking, whether you understand video as a medium (not just a vertical format), and how you communicate to a crew who cannot read your mind.

"Show us something we did not ask for."

Brand: Anything.   Format: Anything.

The Scenario

This is the brief where you show us who you actually are as a creative — not who you think we want to hire. Make one piece of design that you would put in your portfolio forever. The only rule: it has to be real enough to actually run.

Deliverables
→

The work — whatever form it takes. A campaign, a film treatment, a publication, an identity system, a single poster. Whatever it is, it should be complete.

→

A 5-line brief you wrote for yourself before starting. Not a brief for us — a brief for you. Show us how you think before you design.

→

One sentence: what would make this piece fail? The ability to see failure from the inside is rarer than you think.

Also Answer
Why this idea, out of everything you could have made? +
Write your answer here. There were a hundred directions you could have gone. Why this one?
What is the version of this you wanted to make but did not — and why? +
Write your answer here. The abandoned version tells us as much as the final one.
We Are Testing Creative instinct, range, and whether you have a point of view beyond what you are asked to produce. This is the brief that separates people who execute from people who create.
